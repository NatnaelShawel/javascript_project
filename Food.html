Food Combining

Food combining is an approach to eating that works on the premise that our bodies can only digest one concentrated food at a time. Concentrated foods are defined as starches and proteins. So, to simplify it, any foods other than fruits and vegetables.

The digestion of starches (grains, potatoes, and many other roots) requires alkaline conditions, whereas the enzymes that digest proteins thrive in an acidic environment. So, if we eat a starch and a protein together, weâ€™re asking our digestive systems to be alkaline and acidic at the same time.

Poor Food Combinations

There are many examples of poorly combined meals

  • Meat + Potatoes
  • Chicken + Biscuits
  • Spaghetti and Meatballs
  • Fish + Chips
  • Hot Dog
  • Grilled Cheese Sandwiches

Eating any of these combinations requires the starch and protein digestive processes to work at cross-purposes. What essentially happens is that they neutralize each other. Neither the protein nor the starch gets digested properly, which can lead to fermentation, which feeds yeast and fungus.


This chain reaction disrupts the digestion of all the foods we eat.

GOOD FOOD COMBINATIONS

There are many examples of good food combinations

  1. PROTEIN (ANIMAL OR PLANT) + NON-STARCHY VEGETABLES OR SEA VEGETABLES
  2. STARCHY VEGETABLES + NON-STARCHY VEGETABLES OR SEA VEGETABLES
  3. PROTEIN FATS (NUTS AND SEEDS) + FRUITS
  4. PROTEIN FATS + NON-STARCHY VEGETABLES OR SEA VEGETABLES
  5. PROTEIN FATS + SEA VEGETABLES
  6. PROTEIN + FATS OR OILS
  7. LEAFY GREENS - ANYTHING

Food combining is less restrictive than it seems. It calls for a bit of thinking and planning about how, when, and what you eat together.


But, give it a try. You may be amazed at how effective it is and how much better you feel.
